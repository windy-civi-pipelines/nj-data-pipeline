SYNOPSIS

     Authorizes HMFA to issue tax credits for certain purposes.

 

CURRENT VERSION OF TEXT

     As reported by the Senate Community and Urban Affairs Committee on May 6, 2024, with amendments.

  


An Act concerning the issuance of certain tax credits, amending P.L.2020, c.156, and supplementing P.L.1983, c.530 (C.55:14K-1 et seq.).

     (i)  the increased demand for opportunities to create or retain employment and investment in the State as indicated by the volume of project applications and the amount of tax credits being sought by those applications;

     (ii)  the need to protect the State's economic position in the event of an economic downturn;

     (iii)  the quality of project applications and the net economic benefit to the State and municipalities associated with those applications;

     (iv)  opportunities for project applications to strengthen or protect the competitiveness of the State under the prevailing market conditions;

     (vi)  increased investment and employment in high-growth technology sectors and in projects that entail collaboration with education institutions in the State;

     (vii)  increased development proximate to mass transit facilities;

     (viii)  any other factor deemed relevant by the authority.

     (2)  in fiscal year 2024, $250,000,000 for New Jersey studio partners and $250,000,000 for New Jersey film-lease production companies; and

     (3)  in fiscal year 2025, $250,000,000 for New Jersey studio partners and $250,000,000 for New Jersey film-lease production companies.

     2.    (New section)  As used in sections 2 through 5 of P.L.    , c.    (C.        ) (pending before the Legislature as this bill):

     Director means the Director of the Division of Taxation in the Department of the Treasury.

     Low income housing tax credit means a tax credit provided through the federal Low Income Housing Tax Credit program pursuant to section 42 of the Internal Revenue Code (26 U.S.C. s.42).

     Project financing gap means the same as the term is defined in section 55 of P.L.2020, c.156 (C.34:1B-323).

     Qualified project means a housing project that satisfies all eligibility requirements, as determined by the agency, for the receipt of a nine-percent low income housing tax credit, notwithstanding the availability or unavailability of such funds.

 

     3.    (New section)  a.  Notwithstanding any provision of law or regulation to the contrary, the agency may award the total amount of tax credits allocated to the agency pursuant to subparagraph (e) of paragraph (1) of subsection b. of section 98 of P.L.2020, c.156 (C.34:1B-362) in accordance with the provisions of this section.

     b.    The agency may award project financing tax credits to the developers of qualified projects, which tax credits shall supplement the value of any four-percent low income housing tax credits previously awarded to the developer for the qualified project, subject to the provisions of section 4 of P.L.    , c.    (C.        ) (pending before the Legislature as this bill).

     c.     In addition to any other eligibility requirements that the agency may deem appropriate, the tax credits authorized under this section shall be available to any developer of a qualified project that:

     (1)   received a four-percent low income housing tax credit for the qualified project from the agency due to the total demand for nine-percent low income housing tax credits exceeding the total value of such tax credits available for distribution by the agency, as made available to the agency pursuant to section 42 of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986 (26 U.S.C. s.42); and

     (2)   demonstrates the existence of a project financing gap for the qualified project.

     d.    The total value of tax credits awarded to a developer shall equal the lesser of:

     (1)   the amount necessary to ensure that the qualified project receives a total subsidy in an amount equivalent to the receipt of a nine-percent low-income housing tax credit for the qualified project; or

     (2)   the amount of the project financing gap for the qualified project.

     e.     (1)  All tax credits authorized pursuant to this section shall be awarded upon application to the agency, which applications shall be submitted in a form and manner prescribed by the agency.  Notwithstanding any provision of law or regulation to the contrary, the agency may award some or all of the tax credits authorized under this section as part of an existing program operated by the agency on or before the effective date of P.L.    , c.    (C.        ) (pending before the Legislature as this bill) or through the establishment of a new program to effectuate the provisions of P.L.    , c.    (C.        ) (pending before the Legislature as this bill).

     (2)   Upon approval of any application for tax credits pursuant to this section, and upon the satisfaction of such additional requirements as the agency deems appropriate, the agency shall provide the tax credit recipient and the director with a certificate of compliance indicating the amount of tax credits that the recipient may apply against the recipients tax liability, subject to the provisions of section 4 of P.L.    , c.    (C.         ) (pending before the Legislature as this bill).

 

     4.    (New section)  a.  The tax credits issued by the agency pursuant to section 3 of P.L.    , c.    (C.         ) (pending before the Legislature as this bill) may be applied against a developers tax liability under section 5 of P.L.1945, c.162 (C.54:10A-5), sections 2 and 3 of P.L.1945, c.132 (C.54:18A-2 and C.54:18A-3), section 1 of P.L.1950, c.231 (C.17:32-15), or N.J.S.17B:23-5.  To claim the tax credit amount for a privilege period, the developer shall submit to the director the certificate of compliance issued by the agency pursuant to paragraph (2) of subsection e. of section 3 of P.L.    , c.    (C.        ) (pending before the Legislature as this bill).

     b.    (1)  The director shall prescribe the order of priority of the application of the tax credit allowed under this section, together with any other credits allowed by law, against the tax imposed under section 5 of P.L.1945, c.162 (C.54:10A-5) for a privilege period.  The amount of the tax credit applied under this section against the tax imposed pursuant to section 5 of P.L.1945, c.162 (C.54:10A-5) for a privilege period, together with any other credits allowed by law, shall not reduce the tax liability to an amount less than the statutory minimum provided in subsection (e) of section 5 of P.L.1945, c.162 (C.54:10A-5).

     (2)   A developer may carry forward any unused tax credit resulting from the limitations of paragraph (1) of this subsection or from the insufficiency of a tax liability, as applicable, for use in the five privilege periods next following the privilege period for which the credits are awarded.

     (3)   Credits granted to a partnership shall be passed through to the partners, members, or owners, respectively, pro-rata, or pursuant to an executed agreement among the partners, members, or owners documenting an alternate distribution method provided to the director accompanied by any additional information as the director may prescribe.

     c.     (1)  A developer may apply to the director and the agency for a tax credit transfer certificate, covering one or more years, in lieu of the developer being allowed any amount of the credit against the tax liability of the developer.  An application for a tax credit transfer certificate shall not be approved unless the agency affirmatively determines, based on such considerations as the agency deems appropriate, that the sale or assignment of the tax credit transfer certificate is socially beneficial to the State.

     (2)   Upon approval of a developers application for a tax credit transfer certificate, and upon receipt thereof by the developer from the director and the agency, the tax credit transfer certificate may be sold or assigned, in full or in part, for an amount not less than $25,000, in the privilege period during which the developer receives the tax credit transfer certificate to another person, who may apply the credit against a tax liability pursuant to section 5 of P.L.1945, c.162 (C.54:10A-5), sections 2 and 3 of P.L.1945, c.132 (C.54:18A-2 and C.54:18A-3), section 1 of P.L.1950, c.231 (C.17:32-15), or N.J.S.17B:23-5, subject to the carry-forward authorizations set forth in paragraph (2) of subsection b. of this section.  The certificate provided to the developer shall include a statement waiving the developer's right to claim the amount of the tax credit that the developer has elected to sell or assign against the developer's tax liability.

     (3)   The developer shall not sell or assign, including a collateral assignment, a tax credit transfer certificate allowed under this section for consideration received by the developer of less than 85 percent of the transferred credit amount before considering any further discounting to present value that shall be permitted.

     (4)   A purchaser or assignee of a tax credit transfer certificate pursuant to this section shall not make any subsequent transfers, assignments, or sales of the tax credit transfer certificate.

 

     5.    (New section)  Notwithstanding the provisions of the Administrative Procedure Act, P.L.1968, c.410 (C.52:14B-1 et seq.) to the contrary, the agency may adopt, immediately, upon filing with the Office of Administrative Law, such rules and regulations as the agency deems necessary to implement the provisions of P.L.    , c.    (C.        ) (pending before the Legislature as this bill), which regulations shall be effective for a period not to exceed 180 days from the date of the filing.  Thereafter, the agency shall amend, adopt, or readopt the regulations in accordance with the requirements of the Administrative Procedure Act, P.L.1968, c.410 (C.52:14B-1 et seq.).

 

     6.    This act shall take effect immediately.
