STATEMENT This bill re-establishes 6.37 percent as the top gross income tax rate.  Over the last decade, several income tax increases were targeted at the States most productive individuals, families, and small-business owners, causing many within that same tax base to relocate their residence out-of-State. The bill re-establishes the top gross income tax rate to 2004 levels as an initial step toward retaining the talents and contributions of some of the States most productive citizens. ================================================================================ Raw Text: A2665 ASSEMBLY, No. 2665 STATE OF NEW JERSEY 221st LEGISLATURE PRE-FILED FOR INTRODUCTION IN THE 2024 SESSION Sponsored by: Assemblyman  JAY WEBBER District 26 (Morris and Passaic) SYNOPSIS Re-establishes 6.37 percent as top marginal gross income tax rate. STATEMENT This bill re-establishes 6.37 percent as the top gross income tax rate.  Over the last decade, several income tax increases were targeted at the States most productive individuals, families, and small-business owners, causing many within that same tax base to relocate their residence out-of-State. The bill re-establishes the top gross income tax rate to 2004 levels as an initial step toward retaining the talents and contributions of some of the States most productive citizens.
