DEANGELO District 14 (Mercer and Middlesex) Assemblywoman  TENNILLE R. MCCOY District 14 (Mercer and Middlesex) Co-Sponsored by: Assemblywomen Peterpaul, Donlon, Assemblyman Scharfenberger, Assemblywomen Flynn and Haider SYNOPSIS Imposes locational restriction on construction of a new crematory. CURRENT VERSION OF TEXT As reported by the Assembly Commerce, Economic Development and Agriculture Committee on March 20, 2025, with amendments. An Act concerning the construction of a new crematory in certain areas and amending P.L.2011, c.230. Be It Enacted by the Senate and General Assembly of the State of New Jersey: 1.  Section 5 of P.L.2011, c.230 (C.45:27-40) is amended to read as follows: 5.    a.  A crematory shall be located only on dedicated cemetery property not exclusively devoted to: (1) the operation of a crematory; or (2) the operation of a crematory and the disposition of cremated remains.  This [section] subsection shall not apply to a crematorium operated in conjunction with a funeral home pursuant to paragraph (4) of subsection c. of section 16 of P.L.2003, c.261 (C.45:27-16). b.  On or after 1[the effective date of P.L.    , c.    (pending before the Legislature as this bill)] January 1, 20251, the board shall not approve an application for construction of a crematory pursuant to section 4 of P.L.2011, c.230 (C.45:27-39), if the nearest boundary of the proposed crematory site is located within 1,000 feet of: (1)  a public school or a private school, as those terms are defined pursuant to N.J.S.18A:1-1; (2)  an area zoned primarily or exclusively for residential use; or (3)  a park, playground, picnic area, square, monument, beach, waterfront, recreation area, conservation area, or similar place or property. (cf: P.L.2011, c.230, s.5) 2.    This act shall take effect immediately 1and shall be retroactive to January 1, 20251. ================================================================================ Raw Text: A5275 1R [First Reprint] ASSEMBLY, No. 5275 STATE OF NEW JERSEY 221st LEGISLATURE INTRODUCED FEBRUARY 10, 2025 Sponsored by: Assemblyman  WAYNE P.
