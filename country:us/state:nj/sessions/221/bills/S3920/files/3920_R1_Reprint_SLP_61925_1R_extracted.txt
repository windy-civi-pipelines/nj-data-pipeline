Section 1:
S3920 1R [First Reprint] SENATE, No. 3920 STATE OF NEW JERSEY 221st LEGISLATURE INTRODUCED DECEMBER 5, 2024 Sponsored by: Senator  BOB SMITH District 17 (Middlesex and Somerset) Senator  PATRICK J. DIEGNAN, JR. District 18 (Middlesex) Co-Sponsored by: Senators Amato, Singleton, McKnight and Moriarty SYNOPSIS Requires State Police to establish recovery leave policy for troopers presenting proof of pregnancy. CURRENT VERSION OF TEXT As reported by the Senate Law and Public Safety Committee on June 19, 2025, with amendments. An Act concerning law enforcement and supplementing Title 53 of the Revised Statutes Be It Enacted by the Senate and General Assembly of the State of New Jersey: 1.    a.  The Superintendent of State Police shall establish a 1[maternity] recovery leave1 policy applicable to State troopers who present proof of pregnancy. At a minimum, the 1[maternity] recovery1 leave policy established pursuant to this section shall: (1)   establish at least eight weeks of continuous 1[maternity]1 recovery leave after giving birth, with full pay and benefits, without the recovery leave being considered sick leave or deducted from sick leave balances; and (2)   not subject a trooper to residency restrictions or integrity checks, which may be applicable to sick or other medical leave, during the period of 1[maternity]1 recovery leave. b.    The superintendent shall make available information concerning the 1[maternity] recovery leave1 policy established pursuant to this section on the intranet website maintained by the Division of State Police. The information shall include, at a minimum, applicable policies and forms that are required to be completed before and after a period of recovery leave is taken; frequently asked questions; and contact information that allows a trooper to inquire about the divisions 1[maternity] recovery leave1 policy. 2.    This act shall take effect immediately
