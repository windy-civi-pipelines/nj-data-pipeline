A4181 ASSEMBLY, No. 4181 STATE OF NEW JERSEY 221st LEGISLATURE INTRODUCED APRIL 15, 2024 Sponsored by: Assemblywoman  VICTORIA A. FLYNN District 13 (Monmouth) Assemblyman  GERRY SCHARFENBERGER District 13 (Monmouth) SYNOPSIS Requires establishment of minimum staff-to-resident ratios in veterans memorial homes. CURRENT VERSION OF TEXT As introduced. An Act concerning staffing requirements for veterans memorial homes, and supplementing chapter 3 of Title 38A of the New Jersey Statutes. Be It Enacted by the Senate and General Assembly of the State of New Jersey: 1.    a.  Unless otherwise specified in State or federal law, the Division of Veterans Healthcare Services in the Department of Military and Veterans Affairs shall establish a minimum direct care staff-to-resident ratio, based on resident acuity and facility size, for each State veterans memorial home.  A State veterans memorial home shall maintain the ratio established for that veterans memorial home. b.    The division shall review and adjust, as necessary, the direct care staff-to-resident ratio at regular intervals but at least on a quarterly basis. c.     Nothing in this section shall be construed to affect any minimum staffing requirements for veterans memorial homes as may be required by the Department of Health or by a federal agency for staff other than direct care staff or to restrict the ability of a veterans memorial home to increase staffing levels, at any time, beyond the established minimum. d.    As used in this section, direct care staff member means any registered professional nurse, licensed practical nurse, or certified nurse aide who is acting in accordance with that individuals authorized scope of practice. 2.  This act shall take effect on the first day of the fourth month next following the date of enactment, except that the Division of Veterans Healthcare Services in the Department of Military and Veterans Affairs may take any anticipatory administrative action in advance thereof as shall be necessary for the implementation of this act. STATEMENT This bill requires the establishment of minimum staff-to-resident ratios in each State veterans memorial home. This bill requires the Division of Veterans Healthcare Services in the Department of Military and Veterans Affairs to establish ratios, unless otherwise specified in current State or federal law, based on resident acuity and facility size of that veterans memorial home.  Each veterans memorial home will be required to maintain the ratio established for that veterans memorial home. The bill also requires the Division of Veterans Healthcare Services to review and adjust, as necessary, the ratio at regular intervals but at least on a quarterly basis. Nothing in the bill would affect any other minimum staffing requirements as may be mandated by the Department of Health or by a federal agency for staff other than direct care staff or to restrict the ability of a veterans memorial home to increase staffing levels, at any time, beyond the established minimum.
