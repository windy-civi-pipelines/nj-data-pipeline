STATEMENT

 

     This bill requires sex offenders to report the use of Internet identifiers and notify law enforcement of any name change.

     Under current law, a person required to register under Megans Law, P.L.1994, c.133 (C.2C:7-1 et al.) is to provide the appropriate law enforcement agency with information as to whether the person has routine access to or use of a computer or any other device with Internet capability.  The bill would additionally require the person to provide all Internet identifiers the person uses.  An Internet identifier is defined to include all electronic mail, chat, instant message, social networking, application, software, or similar names used for Internet communication.  An Internet identifier excludes all birthdates, social security numbers, and personal identification or PIN numbers unless disclosed voluntarily by the person.

     The bill further requires a person wishing to assume another name to specify whether he or she was charged with or convicted of a sex offense.  If so, the clerk of the court is required to forward a copy of the judgment to the appropriate registering agency, as set forth under Megans Law.

     Upon a change of name by reason of marriage or other legal process, a person is required to notify the appropriate law enforcement agency no less than 10 days after that name change.  A person who fails to notify the appropriate law enforcement agency would be guilty of a crime of the third degree.  A crime of the third degree carries a penalty of three to five years imprisonment, a fine of up to $15,000, or both.

     Under the bills provisions, information about a registered offender made available on the Internet registry would include all known Internet identifiers of the offender and the current and any former name the offender has used or under which the offender may be or may have been known.
