WIMBERLY District 35 (Bergen and Passaic) SYNOPSIS New Jersey Workforce Housing Partnership Act; incentivizes homebuyer assistance to certain employees, provides tax credits for development of certain workforce housing, and provides certain assistance and affordability protections for middle-income residents; appropriates $55 million. CURRENT VERSION OF TEXT As introduced. An Act concerning assistance and protections for certain homebuyers, designated as the New Jersey Workforce Housing Partnership Act, amending and supplementing various parts of the statutory law, and making appropriations. Be It Enacted by the Senate and General Assembly of the State of New Jersey: 1.    (New section) P.L.    , c.     (C.        ) (pending before the Legislature as this bill) shall be known and may be cited as the New Jersey Workforce Housing Partnership Act. 2.    (New section) The Legislature finds and declares: a.     Housing affordability remains a major challenge for middle-class workers, including teachers, first responders, healthcare workers, and young professionals. b.    Employers in high-cost areas within the State continue to struggle to attract and retain skilled workers due to unaffordable housing options. c.     Public-private partnerships can bridge the gap between wages and home ownership by incentivizing employer-sponsored housing assistance through the implementation of workforce housing programs that prioritize long-term affordability while preventing displacement of existing residents. d.    Municipal governments can benefit from a broader tax base through the development of workforce housing and the revitalization of underutilized commercial spaces. e.     Municipalities in which there are underutilized commercial spaces that may be developed into workforce housing pursuant to P.L.    , c.     (C.        ) (pending before the Legislature as this bill) are hereby declared areas in need of rehabilitation, provided, however, that this declaration alone shall not be used to allow any property to be taken or acquired. f.     To address the State housing affordability issues, it is necessary to: (1)   establish a Workforce Housing Assistance Program to provide State grants to match employer-provided down payment assistance and low-interest mortgage options; (2)   offer tax incentives to businesses that contribute to their employees homeownership; (3)   encourage the development of workforce housing by employers; (4)   incentivize municipal participation through zoning flexibility; (5)   ensure affordability protections for low- and middle-income residents; and (6)   encourage private sector involvement through developer incentives and banking partnerships. 3.    (New section) As used in section 4 through section 10 of P.L.    , c.     (C.        ) (pending before the Legislature as this bill): Agency means the New Jersey Housing and Mortgage Finance Agency established pursuant to P.L.1983, c.530 (C.55:14K-1 et seq.). Authority means the New Jersey Economic Development Authority established by section 4 of P.L.1974, c.80 (C.34:1B-4). Eligibility period means the period, as specified in an incentive award agreement, during which a qualified employer may claim tax credits, as such credits and period shall be determined by the authority pursuant to section 9 of P.L.    , c.     (C.        ) (pending before the Legislature as this bill). Low- and moderate-income housing means housing that is affordable, according to the federal Department of Housing and Urban Development or other recognized standards, for home ownership and rental costs and occupied or reserved for occupancy by households with a gross household income not more than 80 percent of the median gross household income for households of the same size within the housing region in which the housing is located. Middle-income means household income below 120 percent of the area median income determined by the United States Department of Housing and Urban Development. Program means the Workforce Housing Assistance Program established pursuant to section 4 of P.L.    , c.     (C.        ) (pending before the Legislature as this bill). Project cost means the sum total of all costs incurred by a developer in the acquisition, development, construction, improvement, or rehabilitation of a workforce housing project, which are approved by the authority as reasonable or necessary, which costs shall include, but not limited to:  (1) the cost of land acquisition and any buildings thereon; (2) the cost of site preparation, demolition, and development; (3) architect, engineer, legal, and other fees paid or payable in connection with the planning, execution, and financing of the project; (4) the cost of necessary studies, surveys, plans, and permits; (5) insurance, interest, financing, and assessment costs and other operating and carrying costs during construction; (6) the cost of construction, reconstruction, fixtures, and equipment related to the real property; (7) the cost of land improvements; (8) necessary expenses in connection with initial occupancy of the project; (9) a reasonable profit or fee to the builder; (10) an allowance established by the authority for working capital and contingency reserves, and reserves for any operating deficits; (11) costs of guarantees, insurance, or other additional financial security for the project; and (12) the cost of such other items, including tenant relocation, as the authority shall determine to be reasonable or necessary for the development of the project, less any and all net rents and other net revenues received from the operation of the real and personal property on the project site during construction, improvement, or rehabilitation.  All components of project cost shall be subject to approval by the authority and audit by, or at the direction of, the authority. Qualified employee means a full-time employee of a qualified employer who has worked for the qualified employer for a minimum of one calendar year prior to the employers date of application for the program, which employee is middle-income or a member of a middle-income household. Qualified employer means a person or business entity doing business in the State, which person or business entity employs one or more qualified employees residing within this State. Relief program means the Workforce Housing Impact Relief Program established pursuant to section 10 of P.L.    , c.     (C.        ) (pending before the Legislature as this bill). Underutilized commercial property means a vacant office building, shopping mall, or other commercial space, which property meets the following criteria: (1)   the commercial space has a vacancy rate of at least 25 percent for a continuous period of at least 18 months immediately preceding the application for development; (2)   the commercial space has suffered an economic downturn over the three-year period immediately preceding the date of the application, as demonstrated by evidence of a quantifiable loss in revenue such that the developers expenses for the premises have exceeded revenues by 30 percent or more each year; and (3)   the owner of the property has made and maintained a continuous, good faith effort to redress the vacancy, actively rent, or actively market the property during the 18 months immediately preceding the application for development. Workforce housing means housing that is affordable, according to federal Department of Housing and Urban Development or other recognized standards, for home ownership and rental costs and that is occupied or reserved for occupancy by households with a gross household income not more than 120 percent of the median gross household income for households of the same size within the housing region in which the housing is located. Workforce housing project means a residential housing project developed or financed by a qualified employer that primarily includes workforce housing and includes a minimum set-aside of 20 percent of units for households earning not more than 80 percent of the area median income determined by the federal Department of Housing and Urban Development. 4.    (New section) a. Within 12 months after the effective date of P.L.    , c.     (C.        ) (pending before the Legislature as this bill), the New Jersey Economic Development Authority, in coordination with the New Jersey Housing and Mortgage Finance Agency, shall establish a Workforce Housing Assistance Program to provide incentives to qualified employers related to the provision of workforce housing assistance to qualified employees.  The program shall consist of the following two components: (1)   the provision of matching contribution grants, pursuant to section 5 of P.L.    , c.     (C.        ) (pending before the Legislature as this bill), for down-payment assistance provided by a qualified employer to one or more qualified employees for the purchases of workforce housing in this State; and (2)   the provision of low-interest mortgage loans and loan guarantees, pursuant to section 6 of P.L.    , c.     (C.        ) (pending before the Legislature as this bill), to the qualified employees of a qualified employer for the purchase of workforce housing in this State. b.    The authority shall accept applications from qualified employers, in a form and manner to be determined by the authority, for participation in the program.  In addition to such other information as the authority may require, an applicant shall demonstrate that the qualified employer has implemented a down-payment assistance program for qualified employees, consistent with the requirements of section 5 of P.L.    , c.     (C.        ) (pending before the Legislature as this bill).  The authority may accept applications for the program on a rolling basis, subject to the availability of funds for the program. 5.    (New section) a. Upon approval of an application submitted pursuant to section 4 of P.L.    , c.     (C.        ) (pending before the Legislature as this bill), the authority shall distribute matching contribution grants, as necessary, to each qualified employer solely for the distribution to each qualified employee for which the qualified employer has provided financial contributions for down-payment assistance, subject to the provisions of this section. b.    To be eligible for a matching contribution grant pursuant to this section, the qualified employer shall have provided each eligible employee a financial contribution of at least $5,000 toward a down payment for the purchase of a workforce housing unit, which purchase occurs within 12 months of the date of approval of the qualified employers application for the program, and which housing shall be occupied as the qualified employees primary residence in this State. c.     Matching contribution grants shall be issued on a rolling basis, during the 12-month period following the date of approval of the qualified employers application for the program, as necessary to provide matching contribution grants for each qualified employee, except that the amount of each matching contribution grant shall not exceed: (1)   for a qualified employer that employs 50 or more employees, an amount equal to the financial contribution paid by the qualified employer to the qualified employee, but not to exceed a maximum contribution of $10,000 per qualified employee; or (2)   for a qualified employer that employs fewer than 50 employees, an amount equal to 200 percent of the financial contribution paid by the qualified employer to the qualified employee, but not to exceed a maximum grant of $15,000 per qualified employee. d.    (1) Within no later than 15 calendar days following the provision of a financial contribution to a qualified employee for down-payment assistance purposes, the qualified employer shall provide written notice to the authority, including such information and documentation as may be required by the authority to substantiate the eligibility for a matching contribution grant.  Upon review and approval of the written notice, the authority shall issue a matching contribution grant to the qualified employer, and the qualified employer shall distribute the full balance of the grant to the qualified employee within no later than seven calendar days following the date of receipt. (2)   All matching contribution grants awarded pursuant to this section shall be issued from the deposits in the Workforce Housing Assistance Program Fund, established pursuant to section 7 of P.L.    , c.     (C.        ) (pending before the Legislature as this bill). e.     The authority shall issue, to any qualified employer providing a financial contribution matched by the authority pursuant to this section, an annual certification of the qualified employers financial contribution paid to each qualified employees purchase of a primary residence in this State and, as appropriate, the total value of the qualified employers financial contributions to qualified employees. 6.    (New section) a. Upon approval of an application submitted pursuant to section 4 of P.L.    , c.     (C.        ) (pending before the Legislature as this bill), the authority shall make available low-interest mortgage loans and loan-guarantees to the qualified employees of the qualified employer, subject to the provisions of this section. b.    A qualified employee of a qualified employer that is approved to participate in the program shall apply directly to the authority for the issuance of a low-interest mortgage loan or a loan guarantee.  The application shall be submitted in a form and manner prescribed by the authority and shall contain such information and documentation as may be required by the authority.  The authority shall approve applications submitted pursuant to this section on a rolling basis, subject to the availability of funds. c.     Any low-interest mortgage loan or loan guarantee shall be issued according to such terms and conditions as may be determined by the authority, provided that such loans and loan guarantees shall only be issued for the purchase of a workforce housing unit by a qualified employee, which purchase occurs within 12 months of the date of approval of the qualified employers application for the program, and which housing shall be occupied as the qualified employees primary residence in this State. d.    All low-interest mortgage loans and loan guarantees awarded pursuant to this section shall be issued from the deposits in the Workforce Housing Assistance Program Fund, established pursuant to section 7 of P.L.    , c.     (C.        ) (pending before the Legislature as this bill), and all loan repayments shall be deposited into the fund. 7.    (New section) a. The authority shall establish and administer a special, non-lapsing fund, to be known as the Workforce Housing Assistance Program Fund, which shall serve as the repository of all funds necessary to support the program.  The fund shall be credited with: (1)   appropriations made by the Legislature; (2)   funds provided by the federal government; (3)   any return on investment of monies deposited in the fund; and (4)   other funds which may be available to the authority for the purpose of implementing the program. b.    Notwithstanding any other provision of law to the contrary, monies in the fund shall be used by the authority for the sole purpose of providing matching contribution grants, low-interest mortgage loans, and loan guarantees under the Workforce Housing Assistance Program established pursuant to section 4 of P.L.    , c.     (C.        ) (pending before the Legislature as this bill). 8.    (New section) a. Within three years following the effective date of P.L.    , c.     (C.        ) (pending before the Legislature as this bill), and every three years thereafter, the authority, in coordination with the agency, shall review the effectiveness of the Workforce Housing Assistance Program, including, but not limited to: (1)   the impact of the program on employee retention in the State; (2)   the rates of participation in the program by qualified employers; (3)   the rates of participation in the program by qualified employers with fewer than 50 employees; (4)   the rates of homeownership by qualified employees in the State; and (5)   any other measure by which the authority determines the success of the Workforce Housing Assistance Program may best be studied. b.    The authority, in coordination with the agency, shall report its findings pursuant to each review conducted pursuant to subsection a. of this section and shall recommend the continuation, modification, or termination of the Workforce Housing Assistance Program, including any recommended changes to funding for the program, to the Governor and, pursuant to section 2 of P.L.1991, c.164 (C.52:14-19.1), to the Legislature. 9.    (New section) a. A qualified employer shall be eligible to receive an incentive award, including the issuance of tax credits, to support a portion of the project costs incurred in the conversion of underutilized commercial property into a workforce housing project.  A qualified employer shall submit an application to the authority, in a form and manner prescribed by the authority, provided that the qualified employer shall demonstrate the following in the application: (1)   the applicant is a qualified employer that seeks to convert underutilized commercial property into a workforce housing project; (2)   evidence satisfactory to the authority that the development of the workforce housing project is in compliance with applicable State and federal laws and safety standards; (3)   the anticipated project costs to be incurred by the qualified employer in developing the workforce housing project; (4)   assurances deemed satisfactory to the authority that a minimum of 20 percent of units in the workforce housing project shall be set aside for low- and moderate-income housing; and (5)   any other information or documentation required by the authority. b.    (1) The authority shall review and approve applications submitted pursuant to this section on a rolling basis.  Upon approval of an application, the authority shall enter into an incentive award agreement with the qualified employer for the development of the workforce housing project. (2)   An incentive award agreement shall specify the amount of the incentive award that shall be awarded to the qualified employer and the duration of the eligibility period.  The incentive award agreement shall provide an estimated date of completion and include a requirement for periodic progress reports, which agreement shall condition the incentive award upon the qualified employers continued compliance with the incentive award agreement and continued submission of progress reports.  The obligations of the qualified employer under the incentive award agreement shall include, but not be limited to, ensuring that any rental rates set aside for low- and moderate-income housing are reserved for low- and moderate-income housing for a minimum of 15 years following the date of project completion. c.     A qualified employer shall submit, prior to the first disbursement of tax credits under the incentive award agreement, but no later than six months following project completion, satisfactory evidence of actual project costs, as certified by a certified public accountant, and evidence of a temporary certificate of occupancy, or other event evidencing project completion that begins the eligibility period indicated in the incentive award agreement.  The qualified employer, or an authorized agent thereof, shall certify that the information provided pursuant to this subsection is true under the penalty of perjury. Claims, records, or statements submitted by a qualified employer to the authority in order to receive tax credits shall not be considered claims, records, or statements made in connection with State tax laws.  Thereafter, the authority shall issue to the qualified employer a certification of the approved project costs for the development of the workforce housing project. d.    In accordance with the incentive award agreement, beginning upon the receipts of occupancy permits for all housing units within the workforce housing project, or upon any other event evidencing project completion as set forth in the incentive award agreement, a qualified employer shall be allowed a total tax credit award of up to 15 percent of the approved project costs for the development of the workforce housing project. e.     (1) Upon the commencement of the eligibility period, a qualified employer shall submit an annual report to the authority indicating whether the qualified employer is aware of any condition, event, or act that would cause the qualified employer not to be in compliance with the incentive award agreement or the provisions of P.L.    , c.     (C.        ) (pending before the Legislature as this bill) and any additional reporting requirements contained in the incentive award agreement or tax credit certificate.  The qualified employer, or an authorized agent thereof, shall certify that the information provided pursuant to this subsection is true under the penalty of perjury. (2)   Upon receipt and review of each report submitted during the eligibility period, the authority shall provide to the qualified employer and the director a certificate of compliance indicating the amount of tax credits that the qualified employer may apply against the employers tax liability. f.     (1) Upon receipt by the director of the certificate of compliance, the director shall allow the qualified employer a credit against the tax imposed pursuant to the New Jersey Gross Income Tax Act, N.J.S.54A:1-1 et seq., section 5 of P.L.1945, c.162 (C.54:10A-5), sections 2 and 3 of P.L.1945, c.132 (C.54:18A-2 and C.54:18A-3), section 1 of P.L.1950, c.231 (C.17:32-15), or N.J.S.17B:23-5, which credit may be first applied in the tax period for which it was issued, in the tax period in which it was issued, or in a successive tax period, as authorized in this subsection, without the need to amend the tax return for the tax period for which the credit was issued, subject to the carry-forward provision in this section. Notwithstanding the foregoing, no more than the amount of tax credits equal to the total credit amount divided by the duration of the eligibility period, in years, may be taken in any tax period.  A qualified employer may carry forward an unused credit resulting from the limitations of this subsection, if necessary, for use in the seven tax periods next following the tax period for which the credits are issued. (2)   (a) The director shall prescribe the order of priority of the application of the credit allowed under this section and any other credits allowed by law against the tax imposed under section 5 of P.L.1945, c.162 (C.54:10A-5).  The amount of the credit applied under this section against the tax imposed pursuant to section 5 of P.L.1945, c.162 (C.54:10A-5) for a privilege period, together with any other credits allowed by law, shall not reduce the tax liability to an amount less than the statutory minimum provided in subsection (e) of section 5 of P.L.1945, c.162 (C.54:10A-5). (b)   The director shall prescribe the order of priority of the application of the tax credit allowed under this section, together with any other credits allowed by law, against the tax imposed under the New Jersey Gross Income Tax Act, N.J.S.54A:1-1 et seq., for a taxable year.  The amount of the tax credit applied under this section against the tax imposed pursuant to N.J.S.54A:1-1 et seq. for a taxable year, together with any other credits allowed by law, shall not reduce the tax liability to an amount less than zero. (3)   (a) A business entity that is classified as a partnership for federal income tax purposes shall not be allowed the tax credit directly under N.J.S.54A:1-1 et seq., but the amount of credit of the taxpayer in respect of a distributive share of partnership income shall be determined by allocating to the taxpayer that proportion of the credit acquired by the partnership that is equal to the taxpayers share, whether or not distributed, of the total distributive income or gain of the partnership for its taxable year ending within or with the taxpayers taxable year. (b)   A business entity that is a New Jersey S corporation shall not be allowed the tax credit directly under N.J.S.54A:1-1 et seq., but the amount of credit of a taxpayer in respect of a pro-rata share of S corporation income shall be determined by allocating to the taxpayer that proportion of the credit acquired by the New Jersey S corporation that is equal to the taxpayers share, whether or not distributed, of the total pro-rata share of S corporation income of the New Jersey S corporation for its privilege period ending within or with the taxpayers taxable year. g.    (1) A qualified employer may apply to the director and the chief executive officer of the authority for a tax credit transfer certificate, covering one or more years, in lieu of the qualified employer being allowed all or part of the credit against the tax liability of the qualified employer. (2)   The tax credit transfer certificate, upon receipt thereof by the qualified employer from the director and the chief executive officer of the authority, may be sold or assigned, in full or in part in an amount not less than $25,000, in the tax period during which the qualified employer receives the tax credit transfer certificate from the director, to another person, who may apply the credit against a tax liability pursuant to the New Jersey Gross Income Tax Act, N.J.S.54A:1-1 et seq., section 5 of P.L.1945, c.162 (C.54:10A-5), sections 2 and 3 of P.L.1945, c.132 (C.54:18A-2 and C.54:18A-3), section 1 of P.L.1950, c.231 (C.17:32-15), or N.J.S.17B:23-5.  The certificate provided to the qualified employer shall include a statement waiving the qualified employers right to claim the amount of the credit that the qualified employer has elected to sell or assign against the qualified employers tax liability. (3)   The qualified employer shall not sell or assign, including a collateral assignment, a tax credit transfer certificate allowed under this section for consideration received by the qualified employer of less than 85 percent of the transferred credit amount before considering any further discounting to present value which shall be permitted.  The tax credit transfer certificate issued to a qualified employer by the director shall be subject to any limitations and conditions imposed on the application of State tax credits pursuant to P.L.    , c.     (C.        ) (pending before the Legislature as this bill) and any other terms and conditions that the director may prescribe, provided, however, that the holder of a tax credit certificate may transfer all or part of the tax credit amount, on or after the date of issuance of the tax credit transfer certificate, for use by the transferee in the tax period for which it was issued, in the tax period in which it was issued, or in any of the next three successive tax periods.  The tax certificate holder or transferee may first use the credit against tax liabilities in the tax period in which it was issued or in a succeeding tax period, as authorized in this subsection, without the need to amend the tax return for the tax period for which the credit was issued, subject to the provisions of this section.  A transferee may carry forward an unused credit for use in any of the next five successive tax periods, and the unused credit shall expire thereafter.  Notwithstanding any provision of this section to the contrary, the amount of tax credits that may be claimed by the transferee in any tax period shall not exceed the total tax credit amount divided by the duration of the eligibility period in years. (4)   A purchaser or assignee of a tax credit transfer certificate pursuant to this section shall not make any subsequent transfers, assignments, or sales of the tax credit transfer certificate. h.    The authority shall publish on its Internet website the following information concerning each tax credit transfer certificate approved by the authority and the director pursuant to this section: (1)   the name of the transferor; (2)   the name of the transferee; (3)   the value of the tax credit transfer certificate; and (4)   the consideration received by the transferor. 10.  (New section) a. There is established in the New Jersey Economic Development Authority a Workforce Housing Impact Relief Program for the purpose of providing grants, in such amounts as may be determined by the authority, to certain long-term homeowners that reside in municipalities impacted by the development of one or more workforce housing projects.  The authority may accept applications for a grant pursuant to this section on a rolling basis, subject to the availability of funds. b.    An individual shall be eligible for a grant under the relief program, provided that: (1)   the individuals household earns not more than 80 percent of the area median income determined by the United States Department of Housing and Urban Development; and (2)   the individuals principal residence is located in a municipality impacted by the development of workforce housing projects pursuant to section 9 of P.L.    , c.     (C.        ) (pending before the Legislature as this bill), and the individual has owned the principal residence for not less than 10 years prior to the date of application for the relief program. c.     An individual seeking to receive a grant pursuant to this section shall submit an application to the authority, in such form and manner as the authority requires.  An applicant shall include, in addition to any other information or documentation which the authority may require: (1)   evidence satisfactory to the authority that the individual meets the eligibility requirements in subsection b. of this section; and (2)   a certification that, to the best of the applicants knowledge, no other member of the applicants household has applied or plans to apply for a grant pursuant to this section. d.    The authority shall establish and administer a special, non-lapsing fund, to be known as the Workforce Housing Impact Relief Fund, for the purpose of providing grants to eligible homeowners pursuant to this section.  The fund shall be credited with: (1)   such monies as may be appropriated by the Legislature; (2)   any return on investment of monies deposited in the fund; and (3)   other funds which may be available to the authority for the purpose of effectuating this section. 11.  (New section) a. STATEMENT This bill, designated as the New Jersey Workforce Housing Partnership Act, incentivizes employers to provide financial assistance to certain employees buying homes, provides tax credits for the development of certain workforce housing projects, and provides certain assistance and affordability protections for low- and moderate-income residents. The Workforce Housing Assistance Program The bill requires the New Jersey Economic Development Authority (authority) to establish a Workforce Housing Assistance Program (program) to provide incentives to qualified employers related to the provision of workforce housing assistance to qualified employees.  The program consists of two components:  (1) the provision of matching contribution grants for down-payment assistance provided by a qualified employer to one or more qualified employees; and (2) the provision of low-interest mortgage loans and loan guarantees to the qualified employees of a qualified employer for the purchase of workforce housing in this State.  Under the bill, qualified employers are required to apply to the authority to participate in the program before the qualified employees of the employer may receive benefits under either component of the program. As defined in the bill, qualified employee means a full-time employee of a qualified employer who has worked for the qualified employer for a minimum of one calendar year prior to the employers date of application for the program, which employee is middle-income or a member of a middle-income household.  Qualified employer means a person or business entity doing business in the State, which person or business entity employs one or more qualified employees residing within this State.  Workforce housing means housing that is affordable, according to federal Department of Housing and Urban Development or other recognized standards, for home ownership and rental costs and that is occupied or reserved for occupancy by households with a gross household income not more than 120 percent of the median gross household income for households of the same size within the housing region in which the housing is located. The bill authorizes the provision of matching contribution grants for distribution to qualified employees who receive a financial contribution of at least $5,000 from a qualified employer toward a down payment for the purchase of a workforce housing unit.  However, to qualify for the matching contribution grant, the purchase of the workforce housing unit is required to occur within 12 months following the qualified employers approval for the program, and the property is required to be occupied as the qualified employees principal residence. Under the bill, the amount of the matching contribution grant is to equal:  (1) for a qualified employer that employs 50 or more employees, an amount equal to the financial contribution paid by the qualified employer to the qualified employee, but not to exceed $10,000 per employee; or (2) for a qualified employer that employs fewer than 50 employees, in an amount equal to 200 percent of the financial contribution paid by the employer to the qualified employer, but not to exceed $15,000 per employee. In addition to receiving grants under the program, this bill authorizes qualified employees of a qualified employer that is approved to participate in the program to apply to the authority for a low-interest mortgage loan or loan guarantee for the purchase of a workforce housing unit, which purchase occurs within 12 months of the date of approval of the qualified employers application for the program, and which housing is to be occupied as the qualified employees primary residence in this State. The bill also provides corporation business tax and gross income tax credits for qualified employers that provide financial contributions for down payment assistance to qualified employees, in accordance with the requirements of the program.  Under the bill, the tax credits are to be issued in an amount equal to 25 percent of the amount of the taxpayers financial contributions, not to exceed $500,000 during a tax period. However, for a taxpayer that employs fewer than 50 employees, an additional amount of tax credits is available equal to 10 percent of the amount of the taxpayers financial contributions paid in excess of $5,000 to each qualified employee during a tax period. Periodic Program Review and Funding of the Program This bill appropriates $50 million to the Workforce Housing Assistance Program Fund, which is established under the bill for the purposes of providing grants, low-interest loans, and loan guarantees under the program. Under the bill, the authority, in coordination with the New Jersey Housing and Mortgage Finance Agency (agency), is required to review on the effectiveness of the program every three years, which review is to include:  (1) the impact of the program on employee retention in the State; (2) the rates of participation in the program by qualified employers; (3) the rates of participation in the program by qualified employers with fewer than 50 employees; (4) the rates of homeownership by qualified employees in the State; and (5) any other measure by which the authority determines the success of the program may best be studied. The authority, in coordination with the agency, is required to report its findings and recommend the continuation, modification, or termination of the Workforce Housing Assistance Program, including any recommended changes to funding for the program, to the Governor and the Legislature. Workforce Housing Development Incentive This bill provides for incentive awards in the form of tax credits, and potentially tax abatements, for qualified employers that convert underutilized commercial property into a workforce housing project.  The bill defines workforce housing project as a residential housing project developed or financed by a qualified employer that primarily includes workforce housing and includes a minimum set-aside of 20 percent of units for households earning not more than 80 percent of the area median income determined by the federal Department of Housing and Urban Development. To receive an incentive award for the development of a workforce housing project, a qualified employer is required to demonstrate the following in an application to the authority:  (1) the applicant is a qualified employer that seeks to convert underutilized commercial property into a workforce housing project; (2) evidence satisfactory to the authority that the development of the workforce housing project is in compliance with applicable State and federal laws and safety standards; (3) the anticipated project costs to be incurred by the qualified employer in developing the workforce housing project; (4) assurances deemed satisfactory to the authority that a minimum of 20 percent of units in the project will be set aside for low- and moderate-income households; and (5) any other information required by the authority. Under the bill, a developer that executes an incentive award agreement with the authority may receive tax credits as authorized under the agreement, subject to the approval of annual compliance reports submitted to the authority.  After issuance of the certificate of compliance by the authority, the taxpayer is permitted to claim tax credits in an amount not to exceed 15 percent of approved project costs, as defined in the bill, for the development of a workforce housing project, which credits may be claimed over the course of an eligibility period, as determined by the authority.  The bill also allows a taxpayer to apply for a tax credit transfer certificate so that all or part of the credit awarded may be sold or assigned to a third-party purchaser. Additionally, the bill authorizes a municipality to adopt an ordinance granting an abatement or exemption from taxation, or both, for property in development for the purpose of workforce housing a developer that converts underutilized commercial property into workforce housing or develops on-site workforce housing for qualified employees.  If such ordinance is adopted, a developer may be eligible for a five-year tax abatement as described in the bill, subject to the requirement to make payments in lieu of taxes to the municipality. Zoning and Permitting Requirements The bill authorizes the adoption of certain municipal ordinances and requires that the adoption of such ordinances be a priority consideration for the Commissioner of Transportation (commissioner) when determining allocations of certain transportation funding for municipal projects within each county. Specifically, the bill provides that under the local aid program, the commissioner is required to determine priority for the funding, after the amount of aid has been allocated based primarily on the statutory formula, based upon whether a municipality has adopted an ordinance that permits the development of certain accessory dwelling units, development of duplexes and triplexes in an area zoned for single-family residential use, or mixed-use development.  The commissioner remains required, as under current law, to consider criteria relating to volume of traffic, safety considerations, growth potential, readiness to obligate funds, and local taxing capacity. Additionally, this bill accelerates the processing timelines for certain permits necessary to the development of workforce housing. Workforce Housing Impact Relief Program The bill establishes a Workforce Housing Impact Relief Program (relief program) in the authority for the purpose of providing grants, in such amounts as may be determined by the authority, to certain long-term homeowners that reside in municipalities impacted by the development of one or more workforce housing projects.  An individual is eligible for the relief program, provided that only one application may be approved in any household in any calendar year, if:  (1) the individuals household earns not more than 80 percent of the area median income determined by the federal Department of Housing and Urban Development; and (2) the individuals primary residence is located in a municipality impacted by the development of workforce housing under the bill, and the individual has owned the principal residence for not less than 10 years prior to the date of application for the relief program. STATEMENT This bill, designated as the New Jersey Workforce Housing Partnership Act, incentivizes employers to provide financial assistance to certain employees buying homes, provides tax credits for the development of certain workforce housing projects, and provides certain assistance and affordability protections for low- and moderate-income residents. The Workforce Housing Assistance Program The bill requires the New Jersey Economic Development Authority (authority) to establish a Workforce Housing Assistance Program (program) to provide incentives to qualified employers related to the provision of workforce housing assistance to qualified employees.  The program consists of two components:  (1) the provision of matching contribution grants for down-payment assistance provided by a qualified employer to one or more qualified employees; and (2) the provision of low-interest mortgage loans and loan guarantees to the qualified employees of a qualified employer for the purchase of workforce housing in this State.  Under the bill, qualified employers are required to apply to the authority to participate in the program before the qualified employees of the employer may receive benefits under either component of the program. As defined in the bill, qualified employee means a full-time employee of a qualified employer who has worked for the qualified employer for a minimum of one calendar year prior to the employers date of application for the program, which employee is middle-income or a member of a middle-income household.  Qualified employer means a person or business entity doing business in the State, which person or business entity employs one or more qualified employees residing within this State.  Workforce housing means housing that is affordable, according to federal Department of Housing and Urban Development or other recognized standards, for home ownership and rental costs and that is occupied or reserved for occupancy by households with a gross household income not more than 120 percent of the median gross household income for households of the same size within the housing region in which the housing is located. The bill authorizes the provision of matching contribution grants for distribution to qualified employees who receive a financial contribution of at least $5,000 from a qualified employer toward a down payment for the purchase of a workforce housing unit.  However, to qualify for the matching contribution grant, the purchase of the workforce housing unit is required to occur within 12 months following the qualified employers approval for the program, and the property is required to be occupied as the qualified employees principal residence. Under the bill, the amount of the matching contribution grant is to equal:  (1) for a qualified employer that employs 50 or more employees, an amount equal to the financial contribution paid by the qualified employer to the qualified employee, but not to exceed $10,000 per employee; or (2) for a qualified employer that employs fewer than 50 employees, in an amount equal to 200 percent of the financial contribution paid by the employer to the qualified employer, but not to exceed $15,000 per employee. In addition to receiving grants under the program, this bill authorizes qualified employees of a qualified employer that is approved to participate in the program to apply to the authority for a low-interest mortgage loan or loan guarantee for the purchase of a workforce housing unit, which purchase occurs within 12 months of the date of approval of the qualified employers application for the program, and which housing is to be occupied as the qualified employees primary residence in this State. The bill also provides corporation business tax and gross income tax credits for qualified employers that provide financial contributions for down payment assistance to qualified employees, in accordance with the requirements of the program.  Under the bill, the tax credits are to be issued in an amount equal to 25 percent of the amount of the taxpayers financial contributions, not to exceed $500,000 during a tax period. However, for a taxpayer that employs fewer than 50 employees, an additional amount of tax credits is available equal to 10 percent of the amount of the taxpayers financial contributions paid in excess of $5,000 to each qualified employee during a tax period. Periodic Program Review and Funding of the Program This bill appropriates $50 million to the Workforce Housing Assistance Program Fund, which is established under the bill for the purposes of providing grants, low-interest loans, and loan guarantees under the program. Under the bill, the authority, in coordination with the New Jersey Housing and Mortgage Finance Agency (agency), is required to review on the effectiveness of the program every three years, which review is to include:  (1) the impact of the program on employee retention in the State; (2) the rates of participation in the program by qualified employers; (3) the rates of participation in the program by qualified employers with fewer than 50 employees; (4) the rates of homeownership by qualified employees in the State; and (5) any other measure by which the authority determines the success of the program may best be studied. The authority, in coordination with the agency, is required to report its findings and recommend the continuation, modification, or termination of the Workforce Housing Assistance Program, including any recommended changes to funding for the program, to the Governor and the Legislature. Workforce Housing Development Incentive This bill provides for incentive awards in the form of tax credits, and potentially tax abatements, for qualified employers that convert underutilized commercial property into a workforce housing project.  The bill defines workforce housing project as a residential housing project developed or financed by a qualified employer that primarily includes workforce housing and includes a minimum set-aside of 20 percent of units for households earning not more than 80 percent of the area median income determined by the federal Department of Housing and Urban Development. To receive an incentive award for the development of a workforce housing project, a qualified employer is required to demonstrate the following in an application to the authority:  (1) the applicant is a qualified employer that seeks to convert underutilized commercial property into a workforce housing project; (2) evidence satisfactory to the authority that the development of the workforce housing project is in compliance with applicable State and federal laws and safety standards; (3) the anticipated project costs to be incurred by the qualified employer in developing the workforce housing project; (4) assurances deemed satisfactory to the authority that a minimum of 20 percent of units in the project will be set aside for low- and moderate-income households; and (5) any other information required by the authority. Under the bill, a developer that executes an incentive award agreement with the authority may receive tax credits as authorized under the agreement, subject to the approval of annual compliance reports submitted to the authority.  After issuance of the certificate of compliance by the authority, the taxpayer is permitted to claim tax credits in an amount not to exceed 15 percent of approved project costs, as defined in the bill, for the development of a workforce housing project, which credits may be claimed over the course of an eligibility period, as determined by the authority.  The bill also allows a taxpayer to apply for a tax credit transfer certificate so that all or part of the credit awarded may be sold or assigned to a third-party purchaser. Additionally, the bill authorizes a municipality to adopt an ordinance granting an abatement or exemption from taxation, or both, for property in development for the purpose of workforce housing a developer that converts underutilized commercial property into workforce housing or develops on-site workforce housing for qualified employees.  If such ordinance is adopted, a developer may be eligible for a five-year tax abatement as described in the bill, subject to the requirement to make payments in lieu of taxes to the municipality. Zoning and Permitting Requirements The bill authorizes the adoption of certain municipal ordinances and requires that the adoption of such ordinances be a priority consideration for the Commissioner of Transportation (commissioner) when determining allocations of certain transportation funding for municipal projects within each county. Specifically, the bill provides that under the local aid program, the commissioner is required to determine priority for the funding, after the amount of aid has been allocated based primarily on the statutory formula, based upon whether a municipality has adopted an ordinance that permits the development of certain accessory dwelling units, development of duplexes and triplexes in an area zoned for single-family residential use, or mixed-use development.  The commissioner remains required, as under current law, to consider criteria relating to volume of traffic, safety considerations, growth potential, readiness to obligate funds, and local taxing capacity. Additionally, this bill accelerates the processing timelines for certain permits necessary to the development of workforce housing. Workforce Housing Impact Relief Program The bill establishes a Workforce Housing Impact Relief Program (relief program) in the authority for the purpose of providing grants, in such amounts as may be determined by the authority, to certain long-term homeowners that reside in municipalities impacted by the development of one or more workforce housing projects.  An individual is eligible for the relief program, provided that only one application may be approved in any household in any calendar year, if:  (1) the individuals household earns not more than 80 percent of the area median income determined by the federal Department of Housing and Urban Development; and (2) the individuals primary residence is located in a municipality impacted by the development of workforce housing under the bill, and the individual has owned the principal residence for not less than 10 years prior to the date of application for the relief program. To fund these grants, the bill appropriates $5 million to the Workforce Housing Impact Relief Fund, which the authority is to establish pursuant to the bill.
