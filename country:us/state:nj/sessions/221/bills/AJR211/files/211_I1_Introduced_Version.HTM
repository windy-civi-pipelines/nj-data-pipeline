A Joint Resolution designating May 18 of each year as Six Triple Eight Day in New Jersey.

Whereas, The United States deployed approximately 16 million military personnel during World War II, including about 11 million in the Army, 4 million in the Navy, and 660,000 in the Marine Corps; and

Whereas, The 6888th Central Postal Directory Battalion of the United States Army, eventually known as the Six Triple Eight, was the only all-African American, all-female unit sent overseas during World War II, consisting of approximately 850 officers and enlisted personnel; and

Whereas, The Six Triple Eight was organized in Birmingham, England on March 4, 1945 and conducted the organization and delivery of military mail at a facility until after the end of the war in Europe; and

Whereas, After discovering warehouses crammed from floor to ceiling with millions of pieces of mail and packages, the women of the Six Triple Eight worked in poor conditions, including no heat and minimal lighting, to successfully deliver the mail and packages that had remained stagnant for at least two years; and

Whereas, Despite experiencing difficult working conditions, racism, and sexism, the Six Triple Eight successfully delivered over 14 million pieces of backlogged mail in just three months to seven million American soldiers and civilian personnel stationed in Europe; and

     Be It Resolved by the Senate and General Assembly of the State of New Jersey:

 

     1.  May 18 of each year is designated as Six Triple Eight Day in New Jersey to honor the 6888th Central Postal Directory Battalion, an all-African American and all-female unit of the United States Army that succeeded at their mission despite numerous hardships.

 

     2.  The Governor is respectfully requested to issue an annual proclamation calling upon public officials, citizens of the State, and other interested groups to observe Six Triple Eight Day with appropriate programs and activities.

 

     3.  This joint resolution shall take effect immediately.

 

 

STATEMENT

 

     This joint resolution designates May 18 of each year as Six Triple Eight Day in New Jersey.

     World War II was a global conflict involving nearly all the worlds countries, with the United States deploying approximately 16 million military personnel.  The 6888th Central Postal Directory Battalion of the United States Army, eventually known as the Six Triple Eight, was the only all-African American, all-female unit sent overseas during World War II, with approximately 850 officers and enlisted personnel.  The Six Triple Eight was organized in Birmingham, England on March 4, 1945 and conducted the organization and delivery of military mail at a facility until after the end of the war in Europe.

     After discovering warehouses crammed from floor to ceiling with millions of pieces of mail and packages, the women of the Six Triple Eight worked in poor conditions, including no heat and minimal lighting, to successfully deliver the mail and packages that had remained stagnant for at least two years.  Despite experiencing difficult working conditions, racism, and sexism, the Six Triple Eight successfully delivered over 14 million pieces of backlogged mail in just three months to seven million American soldiers and civilian personnel stationed in Europe.  Shortly thereafter, the Six Triple Eight was moved to France to complete an identical job of delivering backed-up mail which was completed three months ahead of the established deadline.  On February 27, 1946, the Six Triple Eight boarded the Claymont Victory for its return to the United States and was disbanded at Fort Dix, New Jersey.

     On March 14, 2022, President Biden signed a bill to award the Congressional Gold Medal to the 6888th Central Postal Directory Battalion.  At least 28 women from New Jersey served in the 6888th Central Postal Directory Battalion, including Gladys Eva Debman-Blount and Marion VanOrkey.

     The Allentown Village Initiative will recognize Marion VanOrkey, born in Allentown, New Jersey on May 18, 1914, by hosting a program to tell the story of the battalion and honor those who served as its members.  Since several members of the Six Triple Eight are from the State of New Jersey, it is altogether fitting and proper for this State to designate May 18 of each year as Six Triple Eight Day in honor of the dedicated all-African American, all-female unit of the United States Army that succeeded at their mission despite numerous hardships.
