SYNOPSIS

     Directs BPU to study feasibility of developing advanced reactors Statewide.

 

CURRENT VERSION OF TEXT

     As reported by the Assembly Appropriations Committee on June 19, 2025, with amendments.

  


An Act directing the Board of Public Utilities to conduct a study concerning the development of 2[small modular] advanced2 reactors throughout the State 1[and making an appropriation]1.

 

     Be It Enacted by the Senate and General Assembly of the State of New Jersey:

     2.      As used in this act:

     2Advanced reactor means a nuclear reactor, including a prototype plant, as defined in 10 C.F.R. s.50.2 and s.52.1, with significant improvements compared to reactors operating on December 27, 2020, including improvements such as: (1) additional inherent safety features; (2) lower waste yields; (3) improved fuel and material performance; (4) increased tolerance to loss of fuel cooling; (5) enhanced reliability or improved resilience; (6) increased proliferation resistance; (7) increased thermal efficiency; (8) reduced consumption of cooling water and other environmental impacts; (9) the ability to integrate into electric applications and nonelectric applications; (10) modular sizes to allow for deployment that corresponds with the demand for electricity or process heat; and (11) operational flexibility to respond to changes in demand for electricity or process heat and to complement integration with intermittent renewable energy or energy storage.2

     Board means the Board of Public Utilities.

     2Nuclear reactor means an apparatus required to be licensed by the United States Nuclear Regulatory Commission that is designed or used to sustain nuclear fission in a self-supporting chain reaction.2

     Small modular reactor or SMR means 2[a nuclear fission] an advanced2 reactor that: (1) has a rated electric generating capacity of 2no less than 50 megawatts electric (MWe) and2 no more than 300 megawatts 2electric (MWe)2; (2) 2may be of modular design; (3)2 is capable of being constructed and operated either alone or in combination with one or more similar reactors if additional reactors are or became necessary at a single site; and 2[(3)] (4)2 is required to be licensed by the United States Nuclear Regulatory Commission.

 

     3.    a.  The Board of Public Utilities, in consultation with the Department of Environmental Protection and the New Jersey Economic Development Authority, shall conduct a study and prepare and submit, within 18 months of the effective date of this act, to the Governor and to the Legislature, pursuant to section 2 of P.L.1991, c.164 (C.52:14-19.1), a written report concerning the feasibility of developing 2[small modular] advanced2 reactors throughout the State, including recommendations for the feasibility of, need for, and design of a 2[pilot]2 program for developing small modular 2or advanced2 reactors 2[Statewide]2.
