STATEMENT

 

     This bill provides a $20 million supplemental appropriation to the fiscal year (FY) 2025 appropriations act for the provision of Summer Tuition Aid Grants.  The bill also adds language to the FY 2025 appropriations act providing that eligible students are to receive 75 percent of the full grant award amount for which they are eligible under current law.

     Pursuant to current law, the Higher Education Student Assistance Authority is required to offer summer tuition aid grant awards to eligible students who: received a tuition aid grant award in the immediately preceding fall or spring semester; are enrolled in at least six credit hours during the summer semester; and are enrolled in summer coursework for the same undergraduate program at the same institution of higher education in which the student was enrolled during the students most recent semester of enrollment in the preceding academic year.  The amount of a students summer tuition aid grant is prorated against the full-time tuition aid grant award provided to the student in the preceding semester.  Current law provides that an eligible student enrolled in six to eight credits receives one-half of the value of a full-time award.  An eligible student receives three-quarters of a full-time award if enrolled in nine to 11 credits.

     The $20 million appropriation provided pursuant to this bill for summer tuition aid grants would fund 75 percent of the maximum amount for which students are otherwise eligible under current law.  The summer tuition aid grants authorized pursuant to the bill would fund awards to eligible students enrolled in courses in the summer 2025 semester.
