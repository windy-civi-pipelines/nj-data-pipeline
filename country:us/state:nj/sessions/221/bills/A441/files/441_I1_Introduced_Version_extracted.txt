A441 ASSEMBLY, No. 441 STATE OF NEW JERSEY 221st LEGISLATURE PRE-FILED FOR INTRODUCTION IN THE 2024 SESSION Sponsored by: Assemblyman  JOE DANIELSEN District 17 (Middlesex and Somerset) Assemblyman  BENJIE E. WIMBERLY District 35 (Bergen and Passaic) Assemblywoman  TENNILLE R. MCCOY District 14 (Mercer and Middlesex) Co-Sponsored by: Assemblywoman Murphy SYNOPSIS Exempts experienced firefighters from certain requirements. CURRENT VERSION OF TEXT Introduced Pending Technical Review by Legislative Counsel. An Act concerning firefighters and supplementing Title 40A of the New Jersey Statutes. Be It Enacted by the Senate and General Assembly of the State of New Jersey: 1.    A fire department or force, whether paid, part-paid, or volunteer, shall not prohibit a firefighter who has five or more years of experience in fire suppression, firefighting, or fire rescue from engaging in fire suppression, firefighting, or fire rescue solely on the grounds that the firefighter is uncertified or does not meet a requirement that is not imposed pursuant to statute, rule, or regulation. 2.    This act shall take effect immediately. STATEMENT This bill exempts experienced firefighters from certain requirements.  Specifically, under the provisions of this bill, a fire department, whether paid, part-paid, or volunteer, is not to prohibit a firefighter who has five or more years of experience in fire suppression, firefighting, or fire rescue from engaging in these activities solely on the basis that the firefighter is uncertified or does not meet any requirement that is not imposed pursuant to statute, rule, or regulation.
