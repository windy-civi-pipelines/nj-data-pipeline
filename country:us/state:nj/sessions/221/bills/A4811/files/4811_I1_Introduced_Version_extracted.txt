A4811 ASSEMBLY, No. 4811 STATE OF NEW JERSEY 221st LEGISLATURE INTRODUCED SEPTEMBER 12, 2024 Sponsored by: Assemblywoman  LINDA S. CARTER District 22 (Somerset and Union) Assemblyman  CODY D. MILLER District 4 (Atlantic, Camden and Gloucester) Assemblywoman  LUANNE M. PETERPAUL District 11 (Monmouth) Co-Sponsored by: Assemblyman S.Kean, Assemblywoman Reynolds-Jackson, Assemblyman Wimberly, Assemblywoman Hall and Assemblyman Atkins SYNOPSIS Makes FY2025 supplemental appropriation of $20 million for provision of summer tuition aid grants. CURRENT VERSION OF TEXT As introduced. A Supplement to the annual appropriations act for the fiscal year ending June 30, 2025, P.L.2024, c.22. Be It Enacted by the Senate and the General Assembly of the State of New Jersey: 1.  In addition to the amounts appropriated under P.L.2024, c.22, the annual appropriations act for the fiscal year ending June 30, 2025, there is appropriated from the General Fund the following amount for the purpose specified: 74 DEPARTMENT OF STATE 30 Educational, Cultural, and Intellectual Development 36 Higher Educational Services 2405 Higher Education Student Assistance Authority GRANTS-IN-AID 45-2405  Student Assistance Programs ............................ $20,000,000 Total Grants-in-Aid Appropriation, Higher Education Student Assistance Authority $20,000,000 Grants-in-Aid: 45    Summer Tuition Aid Grants .............. ($20,000,000) Notwithstanding the provisions of section 2 of P.L.2023, c.34 (C.18A:71B-20a) or of any other law, rule, or regulation to the contrary, the amount hereinabove appropriated for Summer Tuition Aid Grants shall be used to provide summer tuition aid grant awards to applicants eligible for summer tuition aid grants pursuant to subsection d. of N.J.S.18A:71B-20 at 75 percent of the maximum amounts for which the applicants qualify pursuant to the provisions of section 2 of P.L.2023, c.34 (C.18A:71B-20a). 2.  This act shall take effect immediately. STATEMENT This bill provides a $20 million supplemental appropriation to the fiscal year (FY) 2025 appropriations act for the provision of Summer Tuition Aid Grants.  The bill also adds language to the FY 2025 appropriations act providing that eligible students are to receive 75 percent of the full grant award amount for which they are eligible under current law. Pursuant to current law, the Higher Education Student Assistance Authority is required to offer summer tuition aid grant awards to eligible students who: received a tuition aid grant award in the immediately preceding fall or spring semester; are enrolled in at least six credit hours during the summer semester; and are enrolled in summer coursework for the same undergraduate program at the same institution of higher education in which the student was enrolled during the students most recent semester of enrollment in the preceding academic year.  The amount of a students summer tuition aid grant is prorated against the full-time tuition aid grant award provided to the student in the preceding semester.  Current law provides that an eligible student enrolled in six to eight credits receives one-half of the value of a full-time award.  An eligible student receives three-quarters of a full-time award if enrolled in nine to 11 credits. The $20 million appropriation provided pursuant to this bill for summer tuition aid grants would fund 75 percent of the maximum amount for which students are otherwise eligible under current law.  The summer tuition aid grants authorized pursuant to the bill would fund awards to eligible students enrolled in courses in the summer 2025 semester.
