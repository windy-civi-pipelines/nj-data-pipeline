Title: S742 Official Title: Number of Sections: 1 Source: versions - Introduced Version Media Type: text/html ================================================================================ Section 1: S742 SENATE, No. 742 STATE OF NEW JERSEY 221st LEGISLATURE PRE-FILED FOR INTRODUCTION IN THE 2024 SESSION Sponsored by: Senator  PATRICK J. DIEGNAN, JR. District 18 (Middlesex) SYNOPSIS Appropriates unexpended funds from New Jersey Bridge Rehabilitation and Improvement and Railroad Right-of-way Preservation Bond Act of 1989. CURRENT VERSION OF TEXT Introduced Pending Technical Review by Legislative Counsel. An Act concerning the improvement of the State transportation system and bridges and making an appropriation. Be It Enacted by the Senate and General Assembly of the State of New Jersey: 1.    a.  Notwithstanding any provision of law to the contrary, any remaining unexpended funds from the New Jersey Bridge Rehabilitation and Improvement and Railroad Right-of-way Preservation Bond Act of 1989, P.L.1989, c.180, which were appropriated pursuant to section 2 of P.L.1991, c.271, shall be reappropriated to the Department of Transportation and made available for the purposes set forth in subsection b. of this section. b.    The funds reappropriated pursuant to this section shall be administered by the department to offset any cost, eligible under the provisions of P.L.1989, c.180, for the cost of rehabilitation, improvement, inspection, and repair of bridges carrying State highways, and bridges owned or maintained by the State, to be allocated for emergency repairs in various counties at the discretion of the Commissioner of Transportation. 2.    a.  Notwithstanding any provision of law to the contrary, any remaining unexpended funds from the New Jersey Bridge Rehabilitation and Improvement and Railroad Right-of-way Preservation Bond Act of 1989, P.L.1989, c.180, which were appropriated pursuant to section 3 of P.L.1991, c.271 and section 2 of P.L.2000, c.11, shall be reappropriated to the Department of Transportation and made available for the purposes set forth in subsection b. of this section. b.    The funds reappropriated pursuant to this section shall be administered by the department to offset any cost, eligible under the provisions of P.L.1989, c.180, for the cost of rehabilitation, improvement, inspection, and repair of bridges carrying county and municipal roads, to be allocated for emergency repairs in various counties and municipalities at the discretion of the Commissioner of Transportation. c.     Any funds appropriated pursuant to the provisions of this section that are not expended or obligated within two years of the effective date of this act shall be consolidated into a single account and redistributed to each county in this State on a formula basis, to be established by the department, for the cost of rehabilitation, improvement, inspection, and repair of bridges carrying county and municipal roads. Funds that have not been obligated, but are required to complete a project under development, shall be subject to consolidation and redistribution. 3.    This act shall take effect immediately. STATEMENT This bill reappropriates unexpended funds from the New Jersey Bridge Rehabilitation and Improvement and Railroad Right-of-way Preservation Bond Act of 1989 (act) to the Department of Transportation (department) that were originally appropriated for the purpose of cost of rehabilitation and improvement of bridges carrying State highways, the cost of rehabilitation and improvement of bridges carrying county and municipal roads, and for the costs of preservation and acquisition of railroad rights-of-way. The bill provides that the funds appropriated to the department are to be administered by the department and be reappropriated to offset any cost to be allocated for emergency repairs at the discretion of the Commissioner of Transportation. ================================================================================ Raw Text: S742 SENATE, No. 742 STATE OF NEW JERSEY 221st LEGISLATURE PRE-FILED FOR INTRODUCTION IN THE 2024 SESSION Sponsored by: Senator  PATRICK J.
