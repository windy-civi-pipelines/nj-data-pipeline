Section 1:
A5618 1R [First Reprint] ASSEMBLY, No. 5618 STATE OF NEW JERSEY 221st LEGISLATURE INTRODUCED MAY 5, 2025 Sponsored by: Assemblyman  CODY D. MILLER District 4 (Atlantic, Camden and Gloucester) SYNOPSIS Requires Administrative Office of the Courts to collect and publish statistical information about consumer debt lawsuits. CURRENT VERSION OF TEXT As reported by the Assembly Regulated Professions Committee on May 15, 2025, with amendments. An Act concerning court statistics for consumer debt lawsuits, and supplementing Title 56 of the New Jersey Statutes. Be It Enacted by the Senate and General Assembly of the State of New Jersey: 1.  a.  As used in this section, consumer debt lawsuit means a civil action commenced in the Superior Court seeking to enforce a contract for consumer credit, as that term is defined under section 1 of P.L.1974, c.146 (C.56:11-1), or to collect a medical debt, as that term is defined under section 2 of P.L.2024, c.48 (C.56:11-57). b.  The Administrative Director of the Courts shall, in addition to any other civil litigation statistics currently being compiled and publicly reported by the Administrative Office of the Courts, compile and publicly report the following Statewide and county-level consumer debt lawsuit statistics: (1) number of new consumer debt lawsuits filed, including the number filed in the Law Division, the Special Civil Part, and the Small Claims Section of the Special Civil Part; (2) number of consumer debt lawsuits currently pending for three months or more; (3) number of consumer debt lawsuits where the defendant is represented by an attorney; (4) number of consumer debt lawsuit judgments granted, including the number of default judgments; (5) number of consumer debt lawsuit dispositions other than the granting of a judgment; and (6) any other statistics concerning consumer debt lawsuits that the Administrative Director of the Courts1[, in consultation with the Director of the Division of Consumer Affairs in the Department of Law and Public Safety,]1 deems appropriate. c.  The statistics compiled pursuant to subsection b. of this section shall be reported annually to the Governor and, pursuant to section 2 of P.L.1991, c.164 (C.52:14-19.1), to the Legislature. 2.  This act shall take effect on the first day of the 1[third] fourth1 month next following enactment, except that the Administrative Director of the Courts may take such anticipatory action as may be necessary for the timely implementation of this act.
