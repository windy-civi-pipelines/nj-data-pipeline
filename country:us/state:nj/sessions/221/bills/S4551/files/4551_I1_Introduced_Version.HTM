Title: S4551 Official Title: Number of Sections: 1 Source: versions - Introduced Version Media Type: text/html ================================================================================ Section 1: S4551 SENATE, No. 4551 STATE OF NEW JERSEY 221st LEGISLATURE INTRODUCED JUNE 2, 2025 Sponsored by: Senator  JAMES BEACH District 6 (Burlington and Camden) Senator  PARKER SPACE District 24 (Morris, Sussex and Warren) Co-Sponsored by: Senators Steinhardt and Henry SYNOPSIS Appropriates $3,750,990 from constitutionally dedicated CBT revenues to State Agriculture Development Committee for grants to certain nonprofit organizations for farmland preservation purposes. CURRENT VERSION OF TEXT As introduced. An Act appropriating $3,750,990 from constitutionally dedicated corporation business tax revenues to the State Agriculture Development Committee for grants to qualifying tax exempt nonprofit organizations for farmland preservation purposes. Be It Enacted by the Senate and General Assembly of the State of New Jersey: 1.  a.  There is appropriated to the State Agriculture Development Committee the sum of $3,750,990 for the purpose of providing grants to qualifying tax exempt nonprofit organizations, as listed in subsections b. and c. of this section, for up to 50 percent of the cost of acquisition of development easements on farmland or for up to 50 percent of the cost of acquisition of fee simple titles to farmland for resale or lease with agricultural deed restrictions approved by the committee, as follows: (1) $3,561,350 from dedicated corporation business tax revenues pursuant to Article VIII, Section II, paragraph 6 of the State Constitution in the Preserve New Jersey Farmland Preservation Fund, established pursuant to section 8 of the Preserve New Jersey Act, P.L.2016, c.12 (C.13:8C-50), made available due to the reallocation of previously appropriated monies; and (2) $189,640 from dedicated corporation business tax revenues pursuant to Article VIII, Section II, paragraph 6 of the State Constitution in the Preserve New Jersey Farmland Preservation Fund, established pursuant to section 8 of the Preserve New Jersey Act, P.L.2016, c.12 (C.13:8C-50). b.  The following projects are eligible for funding with the monies appropriated pursuant to subsection a. of this section: Applicant (Project) Farm County Municipality Amount of Grant Not to Exceed The Land Conservancy of New Jersey Carson, Daniel, & Whitney #1 Warren Frelinghuysen Twp. $2,000,100 The Land Conservancy of New Jersey Carson, Daniel, & Whitney #2 Warren Frelinghuysen Twp. $409,740 The Land Conservancy of New Jersey Carson, Daniel, & Whitney #3 Warren Frelinghuysen Twp. $251,400 The Land Conservancy of New Jersey Harrison, Randy S., & Joann Warren Washington Twp. $177,450 The Land Conservancy of New Jersey Good Springs LLC Warren Franklin Twp. $320,600 c.  (1) Of the monies appropriated to the State Agricultural Development Committee pursuant to subsection a. of this section, $591,700 shall be utilized to provide grants to the qualifying tax exempt nonprofit organizations listed in paragraph (2) of this subsection for up to 50 percent of the cost of acquisition of development easements on farmland or for up to 50 percent of the cost of acquisition of fee simple titles to farmland for resale or lease with agricultural deed restrictions approved by the committee.  Any unused monies provided as grants to the qualifying tax exempt nonprofit organizations listed in paragraph (2) of this subsection may be utilized by the qualifying tax exempt nonprofit organization for allowable project costs. (2) The following projects are eligible for funding with the monies appropriated pursuant to subsection a. of this section, in the amount and for the purposes of paragraph (1) of this subsection: Applicant (Project) Farm County Municipality The Land Conservancy of New Jersey Hubbard, Adam Sussex Wantage Twp. The Land Conservancy of New Jersey Vosper, Michelle Marie Sussex Stillwater Twp. The Land Conservancy of New Jersey 526 Delaware Rd. LLC Warren Blairstown Twp. 2.    The expenditure of the sums appropriated by this act is subject to the provisions and conditions of P.L.2016, c.12 (C.13:8C-43 et seq.), P.L.2009, c.117, P.L.1999, c.152 (C.13:8C-1 et seq.), and P.L.1983, c.32 (C.4:1C-11 et seq.), as appropriate. 3.  This act shall take effect immediately. STATEMENT This bill would appropriate $3,750,990 to the State Agriculture Development Committee (SADC) for farmland preservation purposes.  Specifically, the bill would appropriate funds for grants to one qualifying tax exempt nonprofit organization, the Land Conservancy of New Jersey, for projects at eight farms.  The grants provided by the bill are for up to 50 percent of the cost of acquisition of development easements on farmland for farmland preservation purposes, or up to 50 percent of the cost of acquisition of fee simple titles to farmland for resale or lease with agricultural deed restrictions approved by the SADC. The funding in this bill is provided from constitutionally dedicated corporation business tax (CBT) revenues pursuant to Article VIII, Section II, paragraph 6 of the State Constitution, approved by the voters of the State in November 2014.  The Preserve New Jersey Act, P.L.2016, c.12 (C.13:8C-43 et seq.), implements the constitutional dedication of CBT revenues for open space, farmland, and historic preservation.  The Preserve New Jersey Farmland Preservation Fund was established pursuant to section 8 of the Preserve New Jersey Act.  Of the total appropriated, $3,561,350 is made available due to the reallocation of previously appropriated monies. Of the total appropriated, $591,700 is to be utilized to provide grants to one qualifying tax exempt nonprofit organization, the Land Conservancy of New Jersey, for projects at three farms that were previously funded in fiscal year 2024. The allocations and projects listed in this bill have been approved by the SADC and the Garden State Preservation Trust. ================================================================================ Raw Text: S4551 SENATE, No. 4551 STATE OF NEW JERSEY 221st LEGISLATURE INTRODUCED JUNE 2, 2025 Sponsored by: Senator  JAMES BEACH District 6 (Burlington and Camden) Senator  PARKER SPACE District 24 (Morris, Sussex and Warren) Co-Sponsored by: Senators Steinhardt and Henry SYNOPSIS Appropriates $3,750,990 from constitutionally dedicated CBT revenues to State Agriculture Development Committee for grants to certain nonprofit organizations for farmland preservation purposes.
