AJR200 ASSEMBLY JOINT RESOLUTION No. 200 STATE OF NEW JERSEY 221st LEGISLATURE INTRODUCED OCTBER 21, 2024 Sponsored by: Assemblywoman  SHANIQUE SPEIGHT District 29 (Essex and Hudson) Assemblywoman  MARGIE DONLON, M.D. District 11 (Monmouth) SYNOPSIS Designates first Sunday of May of each year as National Centenarian Day in New Jersey. CURRENT VERSION OF TEXT As introduced. A Joint Resolution permanently designating the first Sunday in May as National Centenarian Day in the State of New Jersey. Whereas, Over the years, the human population and life expectancy of the population of the world have increased, which corresponds with an increased number of individuals attaining the age of 100; and Whereas, According to the United Nations, currently, there are over 570,000 centenarians in the world today; and Whereas, Many countries and cultures offer blessings and gifts to those who attain the age of 100; and Whereas, Additionally, these centenarians are often the subject of news stories highlighting their age, and these stories provide cultural historians and researchers with deep insight into how the rest of society views this elderly population; and Whereas, Centenarians have seen the tragedies of the Great Depression and world wars, the advent of cinema, the golden age of the radio and the invention of television, the innovation of air travel, the exploration of space, and the digital revolution  all in one lifetime; and Whereas, Centenarians will always be with us at each point in history, and all the memories of the lives theyve lived will continue to live on when passed down; and Whereas, National Centenarians Day is a day to celebrate and honor those among us who have lived an entire century and to share in their wisdom, joys, and life as a whole; now, therefore, Be It Resolved by the Senate and General Assembly of the State of New Jersey: 1.  The first Sunday of May of each year is designated as National Centenarians Day in the State of New Jersey in order to celebrate and honor those among us who have lived an entire century and to share in their wisdom, joys, and life as a whole. 2.  The Governor is requested to annually issue a proclamation calling upon public officials and the citizens of this State to observe National Centenarians Day with appropriate activities and programs. 3.  This joint resolution shall take effect immediately. STATEMENT This joint resolution designates the first Sunday of May of each year as National Centenarians Day in New Jersey in order to celebrate and honor those among us who have lived an entire century and to share in their wisdom, joys, and life as a whole.
