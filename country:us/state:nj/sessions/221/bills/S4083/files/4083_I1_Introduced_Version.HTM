SYNOPSIS

     Requires air conditioning in certain residential rental units.

 

CURRENT VERSION OF TEXT

     As introduced.

  


An Act requiring air conditioning in certain residential rental units and amending and supplementing P.L.1967, c.76.

 

     Be It Enacted by the Senate and General Assembly of the State of New Jersey:

 

     1.  (New section)  a.  As used in this section:

     Person with a disability means the same as the term is defined in section 4 of the Fair Housing Act, P.L.1985, c.222 (C.52:27D-304).

     Seasonal rental unit means either a timeshare estate as that term is defined in section 2 of P.L.2006, c.63 (C.45:15-16.51) or a dwelling unit rented for a term of not more than 125 consecutive days for residential purposes by a person having a permanent residence elsewhere.

     Senior citizen means a person 62 years of age or older.

     b.  The owner of a multiple dwelling, that contains a dwelling unit that is occupied by, or intended to be occupied by, a senior citizen or person with a disability shall supply air conditioning adequate to maintain, or an operational air conditioning system capable of maintaining, a maximum inside temperature, in all habitable rooms, bathrooms, and water closet compartments of the dwelling unit, a temperature of no more than 80 degrees Fahrenheit from May 16 to September 30 of each year.  The temperature shall be measured at least one foot away from any surface at the warmest portion of the space subject to regular use by the occupants of any such space. 

     c.  The requirements of subsection b. of this section shall not apply to:

     (1)  any health care facility licensed pursuant to P.L.1971, c.136 (C.26:2H-1 et seq.); or

     (2)  seasonal rental units, provided that the unit owner shall have the burden of proving that the unit is a seasonal rental unit.

     d.  The Department of Community Affairs shall promulgate rules and regulations pursuant to the Administrative Procedure Act, P.L.1968, c.410 (C.52:14B-1 et seq.) to effectuate the purposes of this section.

STATEMENT

 

     This bill requires owners of certain residential rental units to provide for air conditioning or operational air conditioning systems, under certain circumstances.

     First, the bill requires air conditioning or operational air conditioning systems to be available and the temperature maintained at no more than 80 degrees Fahrenheit in multiple dwelling units that are occupied by or intended to be occupied by a senior citizen or person with a disability between May 16 and September 30 of each year.  This requirement would not apply to seasonal rental units or to any health care facility licensed pursuant to P.L.1971, c.136 (C.26:2H-1 et seq.).

     Additionally, the bill requires the Commissioner of Community Affairs to promulgate standards for air conditioning systems in other dwelling units that have an air conditioning system installed.  In those dwelling units the bill requires that, from May 16 to September 30 of each year, the air conditioning system is to be operational and capable of maintaining, in every unit of dwelling space and every habitable room, a temperature of no more than 80 degrees Fahrenheit.  The standards promulgated pursuant to the bill would apply to any multiple dwelling unit.
