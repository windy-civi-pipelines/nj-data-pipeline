A706 ASSEMBLY, No. 706 STATE OF NEW JERSEY 221st LEGISLATURE PRE-FILED FOR INTRODUCTION IN THE 2024 SESSION Sponsored by: Assemblyman  MICHAEL TORRISSI, JR. District 8 (Atlantic and Burlington) SYNOPSIS Establishes young driver program for commercial driver licenses. CURRENT VERSION OF TEXT Introduced Pending Technical Review by Legislative Counsel. An Act concerning commercial driver licenses and supplementing Title 39 of the Revised Statutes. Be It Enacted by the Senate and General Assembly of the State of New Jersey: 1.    a. The New Jersey Motor Vehicle Commission, in consultation with experts in the commercial trucking industry, shall establish a program to increase the number of young drivers who possess a commercial driver license.  The program shall: (1)   assist young drivers in obtaining a commercial driver license; (2)   raise awareness about the benefits of holding a commercial driver license and otherwise encourage young drivers to obtain a commercial driver license; (3)   coordinate with members of the commercial trucking industry to recruit those drivers for employment opportunities; and (4)   engage in any other activities that the Chief Administrator of the New Jersey Motor Vehicle Commission deems to be appropriate to fulfill the purposes of this act. b.    The commission shall include information on the program in the commissions annual report, including data on the number of young drivers that participated in the program in the most recent 12-month period, data on the number of young drivers who have obtained a commercial driver license, employment, or both under the program in the most recent 12-month period, and any other information that the chief administrator deems to be appropriate. 2.    This act shall take effect immediately. STATEMENT This bill requires the New Jersey Motor Vehicle Commission to establish a program to increase the number of young drivers who possess a commercial driver license.  The program is to be established in consultation with experts in the commercial trucking industry. The commission is required to include information on the program in its annual report.
