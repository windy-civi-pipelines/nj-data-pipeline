SYNOPSIS

     Revises certain licensee filing requirements for alcoholic beverage price lists; removes requirement that limited brewery and distillery licensees provide tour when selling certain alcoholic beverages to consumers.

 

CURRENT VERSION OF TEXT

     Introduced Pending Technical Review by Legislative Counsel.

  


An Act concerning alcoholic beverages, supplementing Title 33 of the Revised Statutes, and amending R.S.33:1-10.

 

     Be It Enacted by the Senate and General Assembly of the State of New Jersey:

 

     1.    (New section)  Any licensee or registrant authorized to sell an alcoholic beverage to retailers in this State shall file a current price list as defined and prescribed by the director pursuant to rules and regulations and the provisions of P.L.     , c.    (C.        ) (pending before the Legislature as this bill).

 

     2.    (New section)  a.  (1)  A licensee or registrant authorized to sell alcoholic beverages to retailers who sells less than 60 stock-keeping units per month of an alcoholic beverage may petition the director to file only an initial current price list no later than December 15th preceding the year in which the alcoholic beverage will be sold in this State, except that the director shall have the discretion to establish a different filing date or multiple filing dates during the first 12 months following the effective date of this act.

     (2)   If the alcoholic beverages listed or the price of an alcoholic beverage in the initial current price list filed pursuant to paragraph (1) of this subsection have not changed, further action by the licensee or registrant shall not be required and the prices in the list filed pursuant to paragraph (1) of this subsection shall remain in effect until the licensee or registrant files an amendatory current price list or the initial current price list filed for the following year takes effect.  If the alcoholic beverages listed or the price of alcoholic beverages change, the licensee or registrant shall file an amendatory current price list with the director no later than the 15th day of the month preceding the month in which the change will take effect.

     (3)   If a licensee or registrant who files an amendatory current price list pursuant to paragraph (2) of this subsection continues to sell less than 60 stock-keeping units per month of an alcoholic beverage and the alcoholic beverages listed or price of an alcoholic beverage in the amendatory current price list has not changed after the amendatory filing, further action by the licensee shall not be required and the prices in the amendatory current price list shall remain in effect until the licensee or registrant files another amendatory current price list or the initial current price list filed for the following year takes effect.

     b.    A licensee or registrant who sells 60 or more stock-keeping units per month of an alcoholic beverage shall comply with the provisions of section 1 of P.L.    , c.    (C.        ) (pending before the Legislature as this bill) and all rules and regulations concerning current price list filings.

 

     3.    (New section) The director shall permit a licensee or registrant required to file an initial or amendatory price list pursuant to section 1 of P.L.  , c.    (C.      ) (pending before the Legislature as this bill) to electronically file the price list.

STATEMENT

 

     This bill permits a licensee or registrant who sells less than 60 stock-keeping units per month of an alcoholic beverage to retailers in this State to petition the Director of the Division of Alcoholic Beverage Control (ABC) to file only an initial current price list no later than December 15th preceding the year in which the alcoholic beverage will be sold.  The bill grants the director the authority to change the December 15th filing date or establish multiple filing dates during the first year following the bills effective date.  Currently, ABC requires a current price list to be filed monthly regardless of the number of stock-keeping units of an alcoholic beverage sold per month.

     Under the bill, a price list filed by a licensee or registrant who continues to sell less than 60 stock-keeping units per month remains in effect until the licensee or registrant files an amendatory current price list.  If the alcoholic beverages listed or the price of alcoholic beverages change, the licensee or registrant is to file with the director an amendatory current price list no later than the 15th day of the month preceding the month in which the change will take effect.

     The bill further requires the director to permit electronic filing of an initial or amendatory price list within 18 months following the bills enactment.

     Finally, the bill removes from current law a provision requiring the holder of a limited brewery license or distillery license to provide a tour of the facility when selling the brewerys or distillerys respective products for consumption on the licensed premises.
